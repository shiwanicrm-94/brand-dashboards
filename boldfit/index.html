Red flags

  • No email program. 12.66 lakh addresses unused.
  • SMS and RCS connected but idle. Everything rides on the costliest channel.
  • No campaigns since 19 June.
  • Nothing after delivery. No review ask, no cross sell, no win back.

  • Order updates get 1,800+ clicks a week and carry no offer. Paid for, clicked, earning nothing.
  • Supplements were removed from the store in May 2025. While they were live they produced the best repeat customers of any category (24.3%). Today they sell only on marketplaces.

View 2 · Repeat purchase analysis

Repeat-purchase analysis

TL;DR
  • Lifetime repeat rate is 10.45%: 9 of 10 customers never buy twice. Only 6 to 9 of every 100 new customers reorder in their first 30 days.
  • Half of all second orders come within 12 days. The repeat window is two weeks, not months.
  • ₹63 lakh is abandoned at checkout every week. Only 4 of every 100 abandoned checkouts are brought back. Good programs bring back 8 to 12.
  • Flows that catch buying intent earn ₹18 to ₹75 for every rupee spent. Untargeted blasts lose money.
  • 68% of scored buyers are drifting or gone. There is no win back flow.
  • Order updates (confirmed, shipped, delivered) get 1,800+ clicks a week and earn zero. No cross sell inside them.

The build order

Click any step to see what gets done
1Stop the leaks›
  • Clean the send list so paid sends stop going to dead numbers.
  • Cap the product page nudge. It causes 93 unsubscribes a week.
  • Fix the 1 message per day cap that throws users out of flows midway.
  • Repair or retire the zero return flows: COD to prepaid, collection nudge, homepage flow.
2A/B testing program, from day one›
  • One structured test per week on WhatsApp, in campaigns and in automations. Winner rolls out, next test starts.
  • Offer tests: flat coupon vs percent off vs free shipping vs bundle price.
  • Content tests: short punch copy vs detailed copy, image card vs plain text, hype voice vs coach voice.
  • Timing tests: morning vs evening sends, day of week.
  • Audience tests: RFM tier vs product viewers vs lapsed, to learn who responds to what.
  • Automation message tests: two versions of the checkout nudge, the welcome message and the win back message, always running head to head.
  • Every test logs one line: what we tested, who won, by how much. That log becomes the brand playbook.
3Segment the base›
  • Customers and prospects. Verified live: customer count 1,58,652.
  • Kwikpass identified vs rest of base, to track capture quality monthly.
  • Lapsed 90, 120 and 180 days (Last order date field).
  • 1 time, 2 time, 3 time and VIP tiers. Verified buildable in the platform (Total orders placed field).
  • Suppress dead contacts so delivery recovers.
4Fix the capture engine›
  • Add a real opt in tool (an Alia style popup): offer for consent, phone and email, proper marketing permission.
  • Churn the dead weight: suppress contacts that never engage so delivery and cost recover.
  • Track capture quality monthly: Kwikpass pool converts at 10.7% today vs 18.2% for the rest. The gap should close.
5Welcome flow on Kwikpass join›
  • First hello the moment a phone number is captured.
  • Brand introduction, first order offer, best seller proof.
  • Measure: share of new signups that place a first order.
6Deeper checkout recovery›
  • 3 step sequence: 15 minutes, 3 hours, 24 hours.
  • Separate versions for top seller collections.
  • Target: from 4.3% to 8%+, about ₹10 lakh a month more.
7Win back and 90 day cross sell›
  • Win back starts day 45, not day 90: three quarters of all second orders happen within 45 days. Pools ready: 1.15L beyond 90 days, 70K beyond 180.
  • Win back flow also covers the 58,226 worth saving, sleeping and lost buyers scored by the platform.
  • Subtle cross sell inside order confirmed, shipped and delivered messages. They already get 31 to 39% click rates and earn nothing today.
  • Cross sell ladder fires in the first two weeks after delivery, on the proven paths: bar to bands, shoes to apparel, bottles to gym gear.
  • Product level offers, straight from the data: Ignitr buyers get Volt or Daywalk. Ultimate bar buyers get GripLock or tubes. Chugger buyers get a second colour. Multivitamin buyers get omega 3.
  • 1st to 2nd order ladder inside the first 90 days, then 2nd to 3rd.
8Email warm up›
  • Set up a branded sending domain.
  • 30 send warm up on the cleanest, most engaged segments.
  • Then scale to the 12.66 lakh address base.
9Campaigns restart›
  • 4 to 6 WhatsApp sends a month, segmented only. Never full list blasts.
  • 8 to 12 emails a month once warm up completes.
  • Five buckets: education, cross sell, prospect conversion, offer tests, message tests.
  • Moment calendar: cricket, festivals, launches. One teaser, launch, reminder, recap set per month.
  • Ideal mix: campaigns add 20 to 30% on top of automation revenue.
10VIP, reviews and AOV program›
  • VIP flow for the top 2,186 customers.
  • Review ask after every delivery.
  • AOV messaging tiers from the real curve: 74% of orders are already above ₹1,000, so tiers anchor at ₹1,000 and ₹2,000. Under ₹1,000 gets bundle ups, ₹1,000 to 2,000 gets gift thresholds, above ₹2,000 gets early access.
