View 1 · What is actually there

A healthy engine, running with no driver

BoldFit's WhatsApp account is technically excellent — top-tier sender rating, an enormous audience, automations quietly attributing ₹11–13 lakh a month. But the retention program around it is missing: one channel carries everything (SMS and RCS sit connected but unused), campaigns have stopped, and the customer journey ends the day the order is delivered.

View 3 · The program

What we will do — in priority order

Sequenced by revenue impact and dependency. The principle throughout: fix the leaks first, then capture demand the brand already pays for, then build the lifecycle that turns one-time buyers into repeat customers — while email is warmed up in parallel so the whole program stops depending on a single channel.

The build order

  1. Stop the leaksList-hygiene pass (a third of sends never arrive) · cap the over-firing product-page nudge (93 unsubs/week) · fix the 1-message/day platform cap that ejects users from flows mid-sequence · repair or retire the zero-return flows (COD-to-prepaid, collection nudge, dead homepage flow).
  2. Segment the baseBuild the working segments: customers · prospects · lapsed 90/120/180 days (date-based — verified buildable in the platform today) and 1-time / 2-time / 3-time+VIP purchase tiers (from order history). These become the audience layer every campaign and flow targets.
  3. Welcome flow on Kwikpass joinLakhs of identified visitors (7.84L Kwikpass-identified, 4.83L registered) get no first hello today. Intro + offer + best-seller proof. The cheapest new-revenue flow in the whole plan.
  4. Deepen checkout recoveryFrom single nudges to a proper 3-step sequence (15 min / 3 hr / 24 hr). Base: ₹63L abandoned per week at 4.33% recovery. Every +1 point ≈ ₹2.7L/month.
  5. Win-back + post-delivery cross-sell ladderCovers the 68% of buyers no flow touches today. Cross-sell direction from their own data: equipment → accessory. First-order → second-order ladder, then 2nd → 3rd (cohort nurturing).
  6. Email: 30-send domain warm-up, then campaignsBranded sending domain, warm-up on the cleanest, most engaged segments first, engagement-first content — then scale to the 12.66L addresses. Email becomes the volume channel; WhatsApp goes high-intent only.
  7. Restart campaigns — segmented, never blasted4–6 WhatsApp sends/month on RFM segments + the moment calendar. Full-list blasts are banned by their own results.
  8. VIP + reviews + AOV programChampion treatment for the top 2,186 · post-delivery review collection (fixes the placeholder ratings) · AOV-tiered messaging — under ₹500 gets bundle-up offers, ₹500–1,000 gets threshold gifts, ₹1,000+ gets early-access/VIP framing. Exact tiers set from the Shopify AOV analysis.

The campaign system

~12 sends a month across five buckets, on the agency idea-generation framework
BucketWhat runs in itGrounding
1 · EducationalTraining content in the brand's voice: pull-up progressions, shoe rotation, recovery routines. One idea per send, 3-second hook.Content-led retention is proven in this category
2 · Cross-sellCategory-to-category journeys: bar→tubes, shoes→socks/insoles, massager→recovery gear, cricket→training.Their #1 campaign ever by orders
3 · Prospect conversion~11.8L identified prospects vs ~85K recent buyers. Welcome offers, best-seller social proof (4.1★ × 2.5L+ ratings), browsed-but-never-bought pushes.Largest audience, zero coverage today
4 · A/B — mechanicsSend time, audience (RFM tier vs product-viewer), channel, frequency.One structured test per week
5 · A/B — offer & messageCoupon vs % vs free-shipping vs bundle · hype voice vs coach voice · long vs short.Message-level results tracked per template

Monthly shape: one hero trilogy around the month's moment (teaser → launch → reminder → post-sale), 4–8 evergreen sends with storyline continuity, and the fundamentals overlay — holidays, seasonality, events, cricket calendar (the brand's unfair advantage: RCB sends clicked at 10–13%).

Month 1 — Foundation

  • Fix leaks: hygiene, frequency cap, broken flows
  • 8 working segments live
  • Welcome + win-back flows on
  • Campaigns restart: 4–6 segmented WhatsApp sends
  • Email warm-up begins (30-send ramp on clean segments)
  • Blue Tick application

Month 2 — Email live + ladders

  • Email campaigns begin post-warm-up (8–12/month, five-bucket mix)
  • WhatsApp narrows to high-intent + utility
  • Cohort ladders: 1st→2nd order, 2nd→3rd
  • AOV-tiered messaging goes live
  • SMS onboarding · weekly A/B cadence

Month 3 — Omnichannel steady state

  • Full email structure done; core flows mirrored to email
  • SMS live · RCS onboarding underway
  • WhatsApp dependability lowered — by design
  • VIP program + review collection running
  • Measured against the View-2 baseline: cohort M1 retention, repeat-revenue share, recovery rate
