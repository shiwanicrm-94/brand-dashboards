Executive overview

Five levers that move retention — and the money in each

BoldFit's store is scaling fast: ₹14.8 crore in the first half of 2026. But almost all of it is new customers — retention drives only about 5% of sales, and just 1 in 10 customers ever comes back. These are the five places the recoverable revenue sits, each backed by the brand's own data. Click any lever to jump to the detail that proves it.

1

Browse & cart recovery

In June, ₹2.22 crore of checkouts were abandoned and only ₹6.5 lakh was won back — a 3.45% recovery rate, when it should be 10–15%. Nearly half the recovery messages are never even delivered.

Retention share of sales

Retention drives only ~5% of store sales today. A brand this size running email, WhatsApp and proper flows does 15–20% — closing that gap on the volume you already have.

3

List health

You have captured 12.7 lakh subscribers, but only 1.55 lakh — about 12% — have ever bought. The other ~11 lakh have never placed a single order. A large list is not a healthy list until it converts.

4

Omnichannel, not WhatsApp-only

100% of retention runs on WhatsApp while 12.7 lakh customer emails sit unused. Email costs about 30× less per message — more reach, lower cost, and no single-channel risk.

5

Repeat customers

Only 10.45% of customers ever buy again (on Amazon it is nearly 40%), and there is no flow after delivery today. Lifting repeat to 15–20% is roughly 7,000–14,800 more repeat buyers.
