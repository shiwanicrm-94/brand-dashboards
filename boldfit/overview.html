The goal

Grow retention revenue 2.5–3× — from ₹1.3 crore to ₹3.2–3.9 crore a year

This is the one number that matters. In June, retention brought in ₹10.8 lakh — just 5% of the store's sales. With the levers below, we take that to 2.5–3×. Everything under this headline is how we get there.

The seven levers that get us there

1

Browse & cart recovery

In June, ₹2.22 crore of checkouts were abandoned and only ₹6.5 lakh was won back — a 3.45% recovery rate, when it should be 10–15%. Nearly half the recovery messages are never even delivered.

How: ₹2.22 Cr abandoned × 10% target = ₹22.2L vs ₹6.5L actually recovered = +₹15.7L (₹26.8L at 15%). Source: Kwik Engage GoKwik abandoned-checkout report, 1–30 June.

3

Intentional lead capture

KwikPass grabs everyone who window-shops, so you pay to message a list that's 88% never-buyers. Intentional capture fixes the ratio — fill the list with intent, not noise. Two parts:

  1. On the website — an Alia opt-in popup captures real leads who raise their hand for a reason (offer + consent), instead of KwikPass silently tagging every visitor.
  2. From the marketplaces — give existing Amazon / Flipkart buyers an incentive to come to the website and buy again, turning rented marketplace buyers into owned D2C customers.

Why it matters: a smaller, higher-intent list means fewer wasted ₹0.87 marketing sends and better conversion per send — lower cost, higher ROI, healthier ratio. The panel already holds an "Amazon Purchase Data" list of 3,931 as proof the marketplace bridge works. Source: audit recommendation + Kwik Engage.

4

Omnichannel, not WhatsApp-only

100% of retention runs on WhatsApp while 12.7 lakh customer emails sit unused. Email costs about 30× less per message — more reach, lower cost, and no single-channel risk.

How: WhatsApp marketing ₹0.87/message ÷ email ₹0.03/message = 29×. Source: BoldFit's own Kwik Engage rate card.

5

Repeat customers

You have about 1.5 lakh customers — and nearly 9 in 10 bought once and never came back. Only ~16,000 have ever reordered. The reason is simple: nothing reaches them after they buy. Start communicating, and a large share become repeat buyers.

How: 1,38,790 of 1,54,985 customers (90%) ordered once; only 16,195 (10.45%) reordered. No post-purchase flow exists today. Source: Shopify lifetime order history.

6

Win back the customers who went quiet

About 58,000 of your past buyers are scored "worth saving," sleeping or lost — and not a single win-back message reaches them today. These are people who already bought once; a simple "come back" flow reactivates a chunk of them.

How: RFM scoring of 85,400 buyers — 47.5% Worth Saving + 11.4% Sleeping + 9.2% Lost = 68% (~58,000). No win-back flow is live. Source: Kwik Engage RFM analysis.

7

Restart the broadcast calendar

In the whole history of the account, only 13 promotional broadcasts have ever been sent — and none since 19 June. A 12.7 lakh-person base gets almost no proactive campaigns. A simple 4–8 a month to the right segments is recurring revenue the account isn't touching.

How: 13 campaigns total (₹1.07L), last sent 19 June; category norm is 4–8/month. The 12.7L base is barely marketed to. Source: Kwik Engage campaign log.
