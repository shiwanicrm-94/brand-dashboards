Executive summary

The state of retention, and the handful of things that move it. Internal audit — Klaviyo API (live) · Bik exports · Shopify orders (full history) · Alia analytics. Every figure cross-checked against a second source.

JustHuman's retention is genuinely strong and improving. In June 2026 retention drove 20.0% of Shopify revenue (up from 16% in January), split roughly 58% WhatsApp / 42% email. Repeat behaviour is climbing cohort-over-cohort. The opportunities are not "build the basics" — they exist — they are fix the leaks and steer acquisition into products that retain.

The five things that matter

  • Alia is the capture engine. The popup poll → reward → welcome funnel converts hard: 51,406 polls → 15,157 rewards (29.5%) → 26,773 subscribers, and Welcome-ALIA is the #1 email flow (~₹6L). Protect and scale it.
  • Your acquisition hero doesn't retain. 30-Sec Foot Facial pulls the most first-buyers (10,827) but only 13% come back, vs 25–28% for Microshots / Hair / Sensation Cream. The lever: cross-sell Foot-Facial buyers into those categories.
  • WhatsApp flows leak to frequency-capping. 42–80% of triggers are cancelled/capped on Welcome (56%), Browse-abandon (80%), Checkout-abandon-omni (75%), both Replenishment flows — recoverable revenue sitting in the settings.
  • Dead flows still cost money. "Abandon-cart FF-old" and "Replenishment Cleansing Balm" return ₹0. Switch off or rebuild.
  • WhatsApp campaigns are efficient (27.8× ROI) but a few are pure waste — "Members Only" and "Menopause MS" returned ₹0. Prune the calendar toward the offer/segment winners (Membership 49×, DSC 44×).
Headroom: retention is ~18.7% of revenue today. Fixing the trigger-capping leak, reviving the two dead flows, and steering Foot-Facial buyers into repeat categories are the concrete, data-backed levers to push it higher — without new acquisition spend.

Campaign pattern — what's being sent, to whom, how often

53 email campaigns Jan–Jun 2026, from the Klaviyo API
  • Cadence is 2.0/week (Jan 7 · Feb 13 · Mar 8 · Apr 8 · May 7 · Jun 10). For this revenue band the proven ladder is 3–4/week — headroom for ~1–2 more sends/week if variety improves (sending the same type burns lists, not frequency).
  • Targeting is tight and disciplined: almost every campaign goes to "engaged_45 days" + product-intent segments (ATC Cleansing Balm L30, ATC Microshots L45, product viewers) — typically 2,000–3,000 recipients. That discipline is why delivery is 99.6% and opens run 66–78%.
  • The cost of that discipline: reach. ~2.5k recipients per send from a 40k+ engaged-capable list, and click rate is just 0.76% (benchmark >1.2%) — the list is opening but not clicking. Fix is creative/CTA (hero + one CTA above the fold), not more segmentation.
  • Theme mix skews product-push. Same pattern as WhatsApp: product/launch angles dominate; education, social-proof and use-case angles — which win in their own A/B tests — are under-used.

The funnel fixes, in priority order

this is the most important funnel in the account — signup rate first, then the messaging behind it
  • 1 · Sign-up rate (the multiplier). 7 steps, no skip anywhere, mandatory age, email before phone — and the rate fell 19.3% → 4.33% while views grew to ~57–71k/month. Rebuild: gift → phone/WhatsApp first → email (skippable) → one high-signal question (skippable) → reveal code with expiry. At ~60k views, recovering to ~8% ≈ 5,700 signups/month vs ~3,000 — feeding the best-converting cohort in the account.
  • 2 · Automation messaging (bestseller-led). Alia's own attribution: Microshots = ~65% of popup-signup orders (1,776). The welcome must sell the bestseller explicitly — product story, clinical proof, reviews — not generic brand copy. Carry the dynamic coupon + its expiry in every message (today: zero mentions across all 4 emails).
  • 3 · Collection introduction — missing entirely. No email in the welcome (or any automation) walks the signup through the collections — bestsellers, anti-aging, hair, body. Add a collection-introduction email (bestsellers-first, quiz-answer-matched) so new signups discover the catalogue beyond the one product they saw.
  • 4 · Branch by quiz answer. The answers predict AOV and conversion (Sensitive = highest AOV; Key-Ingredients 30.9% CVR vs Simplified-Routine 9.9%); the segments already exist in Klaviyo — the flow just doesn't use them.
Verdict: Alia is the healthiest part of the retention stack — a high-converting capture engine feeding the #1 email flow, with rich zero-party data that predicts purchase. Fix the sign-up rate first, make the automations sell the bestseller with the code + expiry, introduce the collections, and branch by answer.

The SMS & retargeting build

  • SMS stays on Bik, and only if needed — WhatsApp performs well enough that a second paid SMS platform (Klaviyo SMS) is unjustified cost. If a moment truly needs SMS (checkout fallback, delivery utility), it runs through Bik where the contract already exists.
  • Channel fallback logic: WhatsApp trigger capped → send the email version instead (the 42–80% capped triggers become reachable instead of lost). Email non-opener in 48h on a high-intent flow → one WhatsApp nudge.
  • Retargeting loops: engaged-non-buyers (opens, no click) get the next angle on the other channel; Alia quiz answer decides which product story each channel tells.
  • RCS: pilot only after SMS earns — same numbers, richer cards; India costs have fallen, but prove the audience on SMS first.

What we learned (the audit in 7 lines)

  • Retention is strong and rising (18.7% of revenue, 16→20% Jan→Jun) — the machine works; it's incomplete, not broken.
  • 5 mandatory flows are missing on both channels: activation-per-SKU, cross-sell, win-back, collection-abandon, order-delivered.
  • The acquisition hero doesn't retain (Foot Facial: most buyers, 13% repeat) and the proven fix path exists in the data (FF→Microshots: 547 customers).
  • Zero-party data is collected but unused — 51k quiz answers that predict conversion (Key Ingredients 30.9% CVR vs Simplified Routine 9.9%) feed a generic welcome.
  • WhatsApp flows lose 42–80% of triggers to frequency-capping; two flows earn ₹0 while still costing money.
  • Both calendars over-send product pushes; education/social-proof/restock formats out-earn them per send on both channels.
  • SMS is dark with 33k consented; no channel handoff or retargeting exists anywhere.
