The goal

Grow retention revenue 2.5 to 3x: from ₹1.7 crore a year to ₹4 crore and beyond

Two lifetime targets sit behind this. Take retention from 18.7% to 25% of store revenue. Take the repeat rate from 19.7% to 30%, which is where a skincare brand should be. The store itself is growing 2.5x year on year, so hitting these two targets multiplies retention revenue 2.5 to 3x.

How the math works: June retention was ₹16.8L on a ₹84L store (20%). Share to 25% is 1.3x. Repeat rate to 30% is 1.5x more returning buyers. The store grew 2.5x over last year (₹1.8 Cr to ₹4.4 Cr in H1). Together that is 2.5 to 3x retention revenue. Source: internal tracker + Shopify orders + Klaviyo + Bik.

The seven moves that get us there

Every one comes from a flow-by-flow walkthrough of the account. Each has its proof.

1

Fix the welcome engine

The welcome offer dies in 30 minutes and no message ever mentions it. The popup has 7 steps with no skip button. The WhatsApp welcome fires on first order, not on signup. People decide to buy inside the welcome flow but cannot claim the offer.

Do: extend the offer window, put the code and expiry in every welcome message, add skip buttons, phone before email, fix the WhatsApp trigger.

579 → 117signups last week vs people who entered the welcome flow
2

Rebuild recovery around the bestseller

Alia signups buy Microshots. But browse abandonment does not exist on email, and on WhatsApp it targets haircare, which nobody bought in 30 days. Checkout abandonment has one generic email. Cart has two. The signup offer should follow the person through browse, cart and checkout until they order.

Do: Microshots-first browse, cart and checkout flows on both channels, offer carried throughout.

1,112/weekpeople view Microshots with no email follow-up
3

Stop selling membership to everyone

Only 600 of 24,000 customers bought membership, yet every cart and checkout message pushes it. Prospects are told their "member advantage is included" when it is not relevant to them.

Do: A/B a plain product message with a dynamic code against the membership line. Membership becomes a founder-led flow for 2-time buyers only.

2.5%of customers chose membership; the message assumes everyone wants it
4

Treat the 21,000 prospects as their own audience

Half the list has never bought, and prospects engage with email MORE than customers do. They need trust, not product pushes: the founder, the research lab, why the products are tested.

Do: a reserved monthly slot for this cohort: founder letters, education, limited-time offers, new launches.

21,000prospects, half the audience, engaging but not converting
5

Build the full Microshots framework

The bestseller drives 65% of popup-attributed orders and has the best repeat behaviour, yet has no activation flow on either channel, a generic replenishment, and no catalogue introduction anywhere in the welcome.

Do: activation (how to use, what to expect), reworked replenishment, cross-sell into Sensation Cream and masks, catalogue and founder introduction in the welcome.

65%of popup orders are Microshots; zero activation flow
6

Use the data we already collect

51,000 quiz answers, skin concerns, age, and a Skin Analyser with good submit rates are used nowhere. The analyser's product recommendations never reach the inbox. Opens are also inflated because Apple privacy opens are not excluded in Klaviyo.

Do: analyser results email with recommended products, age-based recommendations in welcome and campaigns, quiz-answer branching, fix the tracking setting.

51,406quiz answers collected, not used in a single automation
7

Win back by skin data

Lapsed buyers already come back when nudged: October buyers who went quiet are placing orders again. There is no win-back flow. We know each person's skin type and concern, so the win-back can speak to it directly.

Do: win-back at day 90 and day 365, personalised to the concern they shared.

38.6%of June orders were repeats and climbing; win-back adds the missing layer

How we execute: five stages: Acquisition, Recovery, Activation, Replenishment with cross-sell and upsell, Win-back. Two phases: first send campaigns to each of these segments and measure, then automate whatever performs. The moves above are the means to the two targets, not separate goals to add up.
