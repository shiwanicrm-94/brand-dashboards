Executive summary

The store is scaling and retention is forming behind it. June did 560 orders and ₹19.0L; repeat rate is 20.2% at 15 months on an AOV of ₹3,580, and repeat orders carry 21 to 33% of monthly revenue. Only two flows convert today (Welcome, Abandoned Checkout), the welcome offer is not explicit on any channel, and the popup signs up 13% of viewers while 26.6% show intent. The one goal is the list; every move in the plan serves it.

Sort the welcome trigger first

The flow fires on "Customer Created" and 591 people entered in 18 days, while the popup collected 415 phone numbers. Before trusting any WhatsApp number, pin down what Kwik Engage counts as a created customer. Names lie; definitions don't.

The gaps, one line each

  • Offer in every message. It is invisible today.
  • Browse abandonment: missing. Build it with per-product messages; Kwikpass recognises the window shoppers we are currently losing.
  • Abandoned checkout: exists. Abandoned cart: missing, add it.
  • Post purchase transactional all present (confirmation, fulfilled, delivered). Cross-sell and upsell after them: missing, and easy with two categories.
  • Campaigns: keep pushing Kwikpass-recognised visitors that their welcome offer is waiting.

Part 1 · Acquisition: grow the list

the main thing; everything else compounds from it
  • Fix the Alia popup first, it is quick: resize the steps, email and phone at stages 2 and 3, fewer questions, skips everywhere, offer visible with a code and expiry.
  • Welcome flow carries the offer explicitly and grows beyond 2 emails, founder note included.

Part 2 · Recovery

email and WhatsApp, because Kwikpass gives us the audience
  • Email: browse split prospect vs customer with the offer until they order; checkout gets a prospect version A/B tested hard; cart makes the offer unmissable.
  • WhatsApp: build browse and cart recovery on Kwik Engage; Kwikpass-recognised window shoppers are the audience we currently lose. Offer in every message.

Part 3 · Activation

the first month owns the second order (median 10 days)
  • First-time buyers get their own post purchase flow: how to care for the product, the founder note, the brand story. Repeat buyers get their own.
  • Care content is per product: the Cloud Tee and the Flow Tee are looked after differently.

Part 4 · Cross-sell and upsell, mandatory

two collections make this the easiest win
  • The proof already exists: the two manual cross-sell campaigns in May clicked 5.9 and 6.8%. Automate them into post purchase on email and WhatsApp.
  • Lead with the second tee of what they love, in the next colour, named. 89% of second orders are the same product and 76% of those rebuys are a new colour: Dark Matter buyers get Deep Water, Moonwash buyers get Deep Water or Dust Rose. Then introduce the other tee.
