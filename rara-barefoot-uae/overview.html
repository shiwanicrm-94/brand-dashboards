The goal

Grow the list first. Everything else already works.

This account is built right: correct triggers on every flow, beautiful emails, strong content, and email already drives 26.4% of store revenue with a 23.6% repeat rate at nine months old. The constraint is a list of 2,324 people. Double the popup's submit rate and every flow downstream doubles with it.

How the math works: the popup gets about 12K views a month and converts 5% to email. Cutting friction (7 screens, no skips, an unused phone question) and strengthening the offer moves submit toward 10%, the level healthy popups reach. Email revenue follows list size here: flow volume already doubled between April and June as signups grew, and revenue followed. Source: Alia analytics + OmniSend exports + Shopify orders.

The seven moves that get us there

Each comes from the account walkthrough. Each has its proof.

1

Cut the popup, grow the list

Seven screens, no skip button anywhere, and a phone question we never use because the account is email only. Fewer steps, skips on everything, drop the phone. Personalisation questions return next quarter once the list can support them.

Proof: 5% submit on ~12K monthly views. Every question costs signups; the phone answer is used nowhere.

5% → 10%submit rate, twice the signups
2

Make the offer land

WELCOME25 is a fixed code with no expiry, shown once and never repeated. Test a stronger offer and the mystery discount format. Switch to a dynamic code with a real deadline, visible in the popup and in every welcome email.

Proof: June welcome revenue sits entirely in email 1 (AED 1,748 of 2,047). The later emails engage and sell nothing.

no expiry → real urgencydynamic code with deadline
3

Turn the 14-day Challenge back on

The Challenge post-purchase flow was the most efficient thing in the account and it was switched off around May 1. For an audience where 73% have never worn barefoot shoes, the challenge is the activation engine.

Proof: AED 24.7 per message, 51% open, 11.7% click. Double the next best flow.

AED 24.7per message, best in account, currently off
4

Split recovery into prospects and customers

Browse abandonment, the highest-traffic flow, sends one sequence to everyone. Prospects are never reminded their welcome offer is still available. The offer must follow them through browse, cart, checkout and campaigns until they order. Customers get their own dynamic code and content that respects they already know the brand.

Proof: 3,463 browse sends in H1 with no split; prospects are 2,005 of the 2,324 list.

2,005prospects never reminded of their offer
5

Fix the first email of every recovery flow

The first email should carry the most audience and the most revenue. In checkout and cart it earns near zero. Test a founder-led first message for prospects and A/B the content until email 1 converts.

Proof: April checkout: emails 1 and 2 earned AED 0, only email 3 sold. Cart Women: zero orders in every month on copy identical to Men's.

E1 = 0today in checkout and cart; it should lead
6

Give campaigns conversion moments

The education calendar holds excellent attention and sells almost nothing. Keep the content spine, add offer-led sends: welcome-offer reminders for prospects, bestseller moments, the mystery discount test.

Proof: 42 campaigns, 49.3% open, 10 orders and AED 3,402 in 6.5 months.

10 ordersfrom 13.2K campaign sends in H1
7

Use the answers we already collect

Every quiz answer sits on the contact as a tag, ready to target. Only the Gym Goers sends ever used one, and they worked. The beginners (never used barefoot) and the pain-driven visitors are the two biggest stories never told.

Proof: 2 of 12 campaign audiences are answer-based. 'Never used barefoot': 2,713 answers, zero campaigns. Pain-driven: 999 answers, zero campaigns.

2 of 12audiences use the data we collect

How we execute: five stages: Acquisition, Recovery, Activation, Replenishment with cross-sell, Win-back. Two phases: campaigns prove each segment first, then the winners become automations. The moves above are the means to the goal, not separate goals to add up.
