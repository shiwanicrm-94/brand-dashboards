The goal

Grow retention revenue 2 to 3x: from ₹11 to 17 lakh a month to ₹30 lakh and beyond

Three lifetime targets sit behind this. Take retention from 21.6% to 30-35% of store revenue. Take the repeat rate from 17.2% to 23%, which the brand's own UAE store already runs. And balance the list from 12:88 toward 60:40 customers to prospects.

The moves that get us there

Every one comes from a flow-by-flow walkthrough of both accounts. Each has its proof.

1

Tell every subscriber their gift is waiting

The quick win, before anything is rebuilt: a campaign to the whole existing list saying ₹500 is credited to your account and has not been used. No signup has ever been told the offer survives past the popup.

Do: one send on WhatsApp and email to all subscribers who never bought, offer-led, with a deadline.

30,866people on the list have never bought
2

Fix the Alia popup: phone first

Alia collects people genuinely interested in buying, while Kwikpass collects window shoppers. Yet the popup stopped collecting phone numbers on 31 May, so every signup since is email-only and unreachable on WhatsApp, the channel Indian customers actually read.

Do: phone number before email, reveal the code after, offer and expiry visible at the reveal.

0%phone submits since 31 May, on ~2,500 signups a month
3

Fix the welcome flow on both channels

The WhatsApp welcome fires on Customer Created, not on signup, so it welcomes buyers while actual signups get nothing. On email the offer sits at the bottom of the message where almost nobody scrolls. In the entire series a new signup is never told about their ₹500.

Do: retrigger on signup, put the code and expiry at the top of every message, more emails explaining why the product is worth the price.

362entered the WhatsApp welcome in June, against ~2,500 signups
4

Rebuild recovery end to end

Collection, browse and cart abandonment do not exist on WhatsApp, while Kwikpass already captures that traffic. The one checkout flow that runs carries no offer, no COD reassurance, no split between prospects and customers, and no product image.

Do: build all four recovery flows, split prospect vs customer, remind prospects the gift is still available, promote COD, put every website USP in the messaging, match email's ₹750 offer.

₹46Labandoned at checkout in the first half of July, almost none recovered
5

Teach the product after purchase

This is a high-ticket product that changes how people walk. First-time buyers need to be taught how to use it, how to break it in, how to care for it. Today post-purchase messages only sell.

Do: an education series on both channels: usage, transition, care, then the review ask.

17.2%repeat today; the UAE store runs 23.6% on the same playbook
6

Cross-sell inside the 10-day window

When a second order happens, half arrive within 10 days of the first: a second pair or another colour. Nothing speaks to a buyer in that window today, and the data already shows which line they trade up to.

Do: cross-sell and upsell timed to the 10-day window, by line and answer.

10 daysmedian time to the second order
7

Win back the quiet customers

More than half the customer base has gone quiet past 90 days, and nothing on either channel speaks to them. Their quiz answers say exactly what they care about.

Do: win-back at day 90+, personalised to the answer they gave at signup.

2,090customers past 90 days quiet, no win-back anywhere

How we execute: five stages: Acquisition, Recovery, Activation, Cross-sell and upsell, Win-back. Two phases: first send campaigns to each of these segments and measure, then automate whatever performs. The moves above are the means to the three targets, not separate goals to add up.
