Campaign pattern · what's being sent, to whom, how often

93 sent campaigns, per-campaign stats
  • Cadence held up on email: May 7 · Jun 8 · Jul 4 in the first 15 days.
  • Aggregate over all sends: 277,459 sent · 168,195 opened (60.6%) · 3,198 clicked (1.15%) · 359 bounced · 2,909 unsubscribed · 9 complaints. Largest sends reach 6,600 to 6,800 recipients, near the full subscribed base.

  • All email, no SMS: zero SMS campaigns ever, despite 14,421 SMS-subscribed contacts.

The phone step died on 31 May. Phone submits ran 10 to 16% of views all year, then 0% from the week of 31 May. Every signup since is email-only and can never enter a WhatsApp flow. Fixing this is Plan Priority 1.
The capture mismatch. Kwikpass collects window shoppers; Alia collects people genuinely interested in buying, and we are not collecting WhatsApp numbers through Alia. The entire WhatsApp list growth is being left on the table, and the current list quality is diluted by window-shopper collection.

The funnel fixes, in priority order

capture feeds the welcome, the welcome carries WhatsApp, WhatsApp carries retention
  • 1 · Restore phone capture. Rebuild the popup phone-first (or phone before the code reveal) so signups are WhatsApp-reachable again.
  • 2 · Deliver the promised offer. WELCOME500 / ₹500 off, with a stated expiry, in the popup reveal AND in every welcome message on both channels.
  • 3 · Recover impressions. Verify the popup shows on all entry pages and re-test the triggers on the live site.
  • 4 · Branch by answer. The three lines map one-to-one onto the "where will you use them" answers, and barefoot familiarity splits education from selling. The segments already exist on both platforms.
